WHY SHOULD WE ALL RECYCLE?


Recycling is important because manufacturers can use recycled materials to make packaging and it helps reduce natural resources from being harvested, thereby lessening CO2 emissions and the amount of plastic waste going into the ocean.


Here are some facts about recycling:

  • The average person in North American creates about 220 pounds of plastic waste each year and 4.40 pounds of waste everyday (according to the EPA).
  • “Americans alone use 500 million straws daily.” (National Park Service)
  • The U.S. creates about 8 million tons of plastic waste that ends up in the ocean.
  • “Americans throw away enough office paper each year to build a 12 foot high wall from Seattle to NY (a new wall every year).” (Recycle Across America)
  • In 2018 the U.S. generated 17 million tons of textiles and 2.5 million of it was recycled and 11.3 million of it went to the landfill. (EPA)
  • Recycling a single aluminum can saves enough energy to power a TV for three hours.


    • Can You Recycle That?


    First, check your item to see what kind of recycle symbol is on it → 1-7.
    Second, if the item has food grease or other food remnants → rinse + dry before recycling.
    Finally, loosely recycle the item (this means do not bag your recyclables). If you cannot recycle the item in your curbside bin find a recycling center near you and see if they will take that item.


    Meaning of Plastic Symbols
    Typically you can recycle plastic bottles and containers, food and beverage cans/containers, paper, flattened cardboard, and glass bottles and containers. Unfortunately you cannot recycle plastic bags/wrap/film, flexible packaging, cups/to-go boxes with wax/plastic coating, and polystyrene foam.

    Polyethylene Terephthalate (PET)
    Typically, drink and condiment bottles are made out of this type of plastic. This plastic can be made into new containers, paneling, and clothing fibers. Five plastic bottles recycled provides enough fiber fill to fill one ski jacket.

    High-Density Polyethylene (HDPE)
    HDPE is commonly found in milk jugs, hair care bottles, dairy product containers, motor oil bottles, detergent bottles, and trash bags. This plastic can be repurposed into drainage pipes, fencing, picnic tables and benches, and floor tiles.

    Vinyl (V or PVC)
    Vinyl is found in piping, medical equipment, some clear food packaging, window cleaner, and detergent.It is usually not recycled, and the small amount that is recycled becomes mats, speed bumps, cables, flooring, and decks.

    Low-Density Polyethylene (LDPE)
    LDPE is found in shopping bags, squeezable bottles, clothing, carpet, furniture, and frozen food and bread bags. It is seldom recycled and can be turned into floor tile, lumber, paneling, bubble wrap, and compost and trash bins.

    Propylene (PP)
    Propylene is commonly found in medicine bottles, bottle caps, straws, and ketchup and syrup bottles. PP can be turned into trays, bins, rakes, brooms, bicycle racks, and battery cables..

    Polystyrene (PS)
    Disposable utensils, egg cartons, and meat trays usually contain this plastic. Many places consider it difficult to recycle, so it commonly isn’t but it can be repurposed into packaging and containers.

    Miscellaneous Plastics
    These items are made out of plastics that don’t fall under any of the other categories like: signs, computer and electronic devices, sunglasses, and DVDs. These plastics are usually never recycled, but they can be transformed and given a new life with a different purpose.

    FAQ
    Why can’t I recycle this, its made of plastic?

    Some plastic items cannot be recycled because they contain other parts that cannot be recycled, or they are made up of a plastic that can get tangled up in the recycling equipment and damage it, or they are too small and cannot be sorted by recycling equipment.

    Can I recycle my disposable mask?

    Single use masks especially ones used to protect oneself against infection or viruses like COVID-19 should be thrown in the trash for public health safety. But if you would like to recycle your Protective Personal Gear safely TerraCycle has a solution!

    How can I make recycling a habit?

    Make yourself a visual poster on what you can/can’t recycle and put in near your recycle bin or print this one. In addition, you can have more recycle bins around your house, seeing them will perhaps make you more motivated to recycle.

    Does recycling really help the Earth?

    Yes, it does! Especially along with the actions of reducing, reusing, and repurposing items before throwing them away. And while it might seem like a hassle to spend an extra couple seconds of your day rinsing out that plastic container in order to recycle it, maybe try it out for a week and the “hassle” could turn into habit.!

    How can I get my community to recycle?

    You can start by educatign yourself about recycling, and share what you learn with your friends and family. If your local restaurants use excessive single use plastics, then you can pitch to them the environmental and economic benefits of using reusable utensils. In addition, you can host a trash pick-up and spread awareness to attendees about properly disposing of waste.
